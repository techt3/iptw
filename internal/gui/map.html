About the Game ▼

You open your laptop. News apps. Social feeds. Streaming. Fifty tabs — and somehow it all feels like the same story, told by the same voices, hosted on the same three clouds.

// system message You are already inside the Matrix.

Ninety percent of global web traffic flows through fewer than ten corporations. What looks like a global internet is a tightly controlled system optimised for engagement and profit — not truth, diversity, or authenticity.

Right now, somewhere out there:

  • A community newspaper in rural Mozambique is reporting on events that will never reach a Western feed
  • A university in Kazakhstan is publishing research that doesn't fit the mainstream narrative
  • A local radio station in the Faroe Islands is streaming music no algorithm has ever surfaced to you
  • A neighbourhood blog in Myanmar is the only honest record of what's happening in that village today

They are real. They are online. Your computer could reach them right now.

IPTW is the red pill. It makes the Matrix visible. Countries you revisit obsessively bleed into Matrix Rain — cascading green code washing over the regions where you are most imprisoned. Every local voice you find is a crack in the wall.
